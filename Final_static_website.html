BharatVote - Trusting the Vote

"We propose a secure digital voting architecture that guarantees voter anonymity, transparent auditing, and cryptographic proof of vote inclusion using hashing, nullifiers, and Merkle tree verification."

Problem Statement

  • Digital voting systems often rely on centralized trust.
  • Voter anonymity and transparency are rarely balanced together.
  • Duplicate voting and ghost voter risks exist.
  • Lack of verifiable proof for individual voters.
  • Need for tamper-evident and publicly auditable election systems.

Literature Review / Market Research

This work studies secure online voting, blockchain-inspired election integrity, and end-to-end verification systems to extract practical mechanisms for real-world deployment.

Research Gap / Innovation

Existing systems focus either on anonymity or transparency. Very few combine cryptographic identity verification, nullifier-based duplicate prevention, and Merkle-tree inclusion proof in a practical architecture. BharatVote addresses this hybrid research gap.

System Methodology

Identity & Registration Layer

  • One-time voter registration
  • Aadhaar, Voter ID, Mobile verification
  • Image capture and declaration validation
  • SHA-256 hashing of sensitive fields
  • Duplicate prevention using hashed constraints

Voting & Cryptographic Layer

  • 256-bit secret_key generation
  • Nullifier = hash(secret_key + election_id)
  • Duplicate vote rejection
  • Encrypted vote storage
  • Merkle tree vote hashing
  • Immutable append-only log

Security & Verification Layer

  • OTP verification (Fast2SMS integration)
  • Live face verification
  • 1-minute secure voting timer
  • Session-based authentication
  • Public audit dashboard
  • Merkle proof-based receipt
